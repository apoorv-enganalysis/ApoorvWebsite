Mass-Spring-Damper System Analysis

Published on December 2024 • 12 min read

This interactive simulation demonstrates the fundamental principles of vibration analysis and dynamics. The mass-spring-damper system is one of the most important models in mechanical engineering, used to understand vibration, resonance, and dynamic response.

System Parameters

  • Mass (m): The inertial element that stores kinetic energy
  • Spring Constant (k): The stiffness that stores potential energy
  • Damping Coefficient (c): The energy dissipation mechanism
  • Force Amplitude (F₀): The magnitude of the external forcing
  • Forcing Frequency (ω): The frequency of the external excitation

Mathematical Model

The system is governed by the second-order differential equation:

mẍ + cẋ + kx = F₀sin(ωt)

Frequency Response

The transfer function of the system is:

H(ω) = 1 / (-mω² + jcω + k)

This creates a frequency response with:

  • Natural Frequency: ωₙ = √(k/m)
  • Damping Ratio: ζ = c/(2√(mk))
  • Resonance: Peak response when ω ≈ ωₙ

Key Observations

  • When damping is low, the system shows strong resonance near the natural frequency
  • Higher mass reduces the natural frequency and makes the system more sluggish
  • Stiffer springs increase natural frequency and reduce displacement
  • Damping reduces peak response and broadens the frequency response

Engineering Applications

  • Vibration isolation systems for sensitive equipment
  • Shock absorbers in automotive and aerospace applications
  • Structural dynamics analysis for buildings and bridges
  • Control system design for precision positioning

Analysis Methods

Modal Analysis

Determines natural frequencies and mode shapes

Harmonic Analysis

Studies steady-state response to periodic excitation

Transient Analysis

Analyzes time-domain response to arbitrary loading

Random Vibration

Evaluates response to stochastic excitation
